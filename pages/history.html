History of Computers

Computers have evolved from simple mechanical calculators to powerful modern devices. Starting from Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine to today's quantum computers.

Key Milestones in Computer History

  • 1837: Charles Babbage designs the Analytical Engine, a mechanical general-purpose computer.
  • 1936: Alan Turing introduces the concept of a universal machine, laying the foundation for modern computing.
  • 1943-1944: The Colossus, the first programmable digital computer, is developed in the UK.
  • 1971: Intel releases the first microprocessor, the 4004.
  • 1981: IBM launches the first personal computer (PC).
  • 2000s: The rise of smartphones and cloud computing revolutionizes technology.
  • Present: Quantum computing and AI are at the forefront of innovation.
